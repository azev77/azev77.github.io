--- layout: archive title: "Simpson's Paradox is a Special Case of Omitted Variable Bias (OVB)" permalink: /Simpson_OVB.html author_profile: true --- Suppose the true model is: $y_{i} = \beta_{0} + \beta_{x} x_{i} + \beta_{z} z_{i} + \nu_{i}$
Suppose you omit $z_{i}$ and instead estimate: $y_{i} = \beta_{0} + \beta_{x} x_{i} + u_{i} \Rightarrow u_{i} = \beta_{z} z_{i} + \nu_{i} $
$X \sim N\left(\mu, \sigma^2 \right)$
